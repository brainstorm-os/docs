## What it can do

- List, icon-list, grid, and gallery views, remembered per folder.
- Smart folders capture a search's query and scope as a live sidebar entry.
- Upload real files through the OS picker or drag them in from Finder — bytes, MIME type, size, and SHA-256 hash included.
- Multi-select bulk delete, move, copy, and rename with collision-safe naming.
- An inspector with Preview, Properties, and Links tabs; folder covers and icons are click-to-edit.
- Virtualized lists keep large folders fast; breadcrumbs, grouping, and a Recently Deleted view keep them navigable.

## Files are objects too

A file you add becomes an [object](/concepts/objects/) in your vault: it can be linked from a note with `@`, given properties, collected into sets, and surfaced in the [Graph](/apps/graph/). Your documents stop being loose attachments and become part of the connected workspace.

## Browsing and previewing

Files gives you a familiar browser for what's in your vault, and the **Preview** app renders supported documents and media inline so you can read without leaving Brainstorm.

## On your disk

Files you add are stored in your vault on your own disk and protected by your vault key. They sync with everything else when you [enable sync](/concepts/local-first-and-sync/), end-to-end encrypted.
